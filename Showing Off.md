#Lesson 79 Showing Off

###Reading

Many people **are fond of** showing off. They always **insist on** **footing the bill** when they are out with friends. They always think of **impressing** people with their **wealth**. They **seem to** think that money can buy friends. In fact, it can't. **Instead of** trying to show off, they should be **themselves**.

---

**be fond of...** 喜歡 **fond** [fɑnd] 喜歡；(adj.)喜歡的，溫柔的，寵愛的    
My mother is not only fond of cooking; she's also a wonderful cool.    
我媽媽不只喜歡烹飪，他還是很棒的廚師。    

**insist on + 動名詞** 堅持要...      
**insist** [ɪn'sɪst] 咬定；(vi.)堅持；(vt.)堅持        
Whenever I take my son out to dinner, he insists on eating hamburgers.   
每當我帶我兒子外出晚餐時，他總是堅持要吃漢堡。   

**foot the bill** 付帳    
= pay the bill   
He didn't have enough money to foot the bill.   
他沒有足夠的錢付帳。   

**impress + 人 + with + 事物** 以某事物使人印象深刻   
**impress** [ɪm'prɛs] 印；(n.)印象，特徵；(vi.)給人印象；(vt.)蓋印，強徵，傳送，給予某人深刻印象   
Bob impressed us with his knowledge of world history.   
Bob對世界史的知識使我們印象深刻。   

**wealth** [wɛlθ] 財富；(n.)財富，大量，富有   
The king has great wealth.       
這個國王擁有大筆的財富。   

**seem to + 原形動詞**　似乎...   
**seem** [sim] 似乎；(vi.)似乎，像是，裝作   
John seems to be happy, but I can't be sure.   
約翰似乎很快樂，但我不能肯定。

**instead of + 動名詞**　非但不...反而   
**instead** [ɪn'stɛd] 代替；(adv.)代替，反而，相反   
Instead of riding bicycles, let's go for a walks.   
咱們不要騎自行車，改去散步吧。

**be oneself** 做真實的自我   
**oneself**　[wʌn'sɛlf]　自己；(pron.)自己，親自   
**yourself**　[jɔr'sɛlf]　你自己；(pron.)你自己   
**themselves**　[ðɛm'sɛlvz]　他們自己；(pron.)他們自己，他們親自   
Don't be nervous about your interview; just be yourself and you'll do fine.   
別擔心你的面試，你只要表現出真實的自我就會做得很好。
